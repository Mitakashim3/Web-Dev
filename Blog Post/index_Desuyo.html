CS Elective

A Computer Science Elective refers to a course or a set of courses within a computer science curriculum that students can choose based on their interests or career goals. These electives typically cover specialized topics within computer science that may not be part of the core curriculum but provide students with opportunities to delve deeper into specific areas of study. Examples of computer science electives might include courses in artificial intelligence, machine learning, cybersecurity, database systems, computer graphics, software engineering, bioinformatics, robotics, and more. The specific electives offered can vary depending on the university or educational institution and their faculty expertise. Students often choose electives based on their personal interests, career aspirations, or to complement their major coursework. These courses allow students to explore diverse areas of computer science, gain additional skills and knowledge, and tailor their education to match their individual goals and interests.
